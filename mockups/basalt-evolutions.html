BASALT — Four Evolutions

Direction locked: the obsidian monolith. Every variation keeps the soul — slanted volcanic glass, cursor parallax & specular, molten amber, heavy rare motion — and evolves a different axis: function, drama, depth, or craft. Hover for parallax/specular, click each stage for its time-advance signature.

C1

BASALT·PRIME — The Conduit

The refined original — tighter bevels (double-edge: bright lip over dark undercut), an etched serial, an amber cap-line at the prow — and one big idea: the inlay becomes an instrument. The molten channel along the foot is the day itself — it fills as watches pass, stretch marks notched along its length, a white-hot bead riding the leading edge. The bar wears its data as jewelry.

C2

BASALT·RIFT — Tectonics

The monolith fractures into tectonic plates — each system rides its own slab at a slightly different skew and elevation, and between them run living molten fissures, jagged cracks glowing from inside the rock, breathing slowly. Embers pool at the plate feet. Time advancing is seismic: the plates jolt apart, the fissures flare white-hot, and the slab settles. A doom clock or terminal resource would literally widen the rift.

C3

BASALT·DEPTH — The Dark Crystal

Evolve the stone into deep crystal. The slab turns translucent: three visible strata inside — a blurred runic bed, a drifting survey grid, the sharp dial face — each at its own parallax depth. Light caustics swim slowly through the interior like sun through dark water, a volumetric amber core breathes behind the clock, and a prismatic hairline refracts along the top bevel. Time advances as a core pulse: the beam flares wide and the new digits condense out of the light.

C4

BASALT·FORGE — The Machined Billet

The monolith comes back from the machine shop. A chamfered billet (all four corners cut 45°, fine brushed grain) with deep milled channels separating sections, labels engraved into the metal and filled with luminous ink that only lights when its section is live, and an amber circuit wire running the foot of the bar with a junction at every section. On advance a pulse travels the wire, lights each engraving in sequence, the hero digits flip on a machined drum, and a single glint sweeps the surface. A hazard-striped chamfer marks the danger corner.
